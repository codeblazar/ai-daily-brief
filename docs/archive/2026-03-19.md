## Today in One Minute

- **LangChain rolls out Polly**: Polly, their new lightweight agent, is now generally available, making it easier to integrate AI capabilities directly into workflows.

## What Happened

### Polly goes GA everywhere in LangSmith  
LangChain announced **Polly is generally available** across all LangSmith environments. Polly provides a streamlined, context-aware agent experience enabling safe, secure, and flexible AI workflows, blending agent reasoning with sandboxed code execution.  

### Two-Stage Hurdle Models for Zero-Inflated Data  
A recent article on Towards Data Science breaks down the **two-stage hurdle model**, a statistical technique for predicting outcomes with excess zeros — think insurance claims or customer churn where non-events dominate. It separates the prediction into a "zero vs. non-zero" stage, then models the non-zero outcomes separately, increasing accuracy and interpretability.  

### Nvidia’s Hybrid Model Nemotron 3 Nano 4B  
NVIDIA and Hugging Face unveiled **Nemotron 3 Nano 4B**, a compact hybrid model designed for efficient local AI on edge devices. It balances compute efficiency and performance to support real-time inference without cloud dependency.

### OpenAI’s GPT-5.4 mini and nano  
OpenAI introduced **GPT-5.4 mini and nano**, smaller variants of GPT tuned to work on lightweight hardware and embedded systems, unlocking possibilities for faster, offline AI applications with lower latency and privacy advantages.

---

## Why It Matters

- **Bridging usability and security**: Polly’s availability with secure sandboxes shows a maturing trend of blending powerful AI with safe execution environments — essential for real-world enterprise adoption.
- **Better predictive models for real-world data**: Zero-inflated data is everywhere but tricky to model well. The two-stage hurdle approach offers practical guidance for improving forecasts and decision-making in domains where "no event" is common.
- **Smaller models, bigger impact**: With Nemotron 3 Nano 4B and GPT-5.4 mini/nano, AI is moving closer to where data is generated — on-device and at the edge — reducing costs, latency, and privacy risks compared to always cloud-based AI.
- **Empowering instructors and practitioners**: Understanding these trends equips you to teach more relevant skills and build systems that leverage next-gen AI capabilities effectively.
