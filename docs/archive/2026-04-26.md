# AI Daily Brief – 2026-04-26

## Today in One Minute

- **OpenAI launches GPT-5.5**, powering Codex on NVIDIA’s new AI infrastructure, improving code generation and automation capabilities.
- **DeepSeek-V4 offers agents a million-token context window**, enabling much longer and richer conversations or document analysis.
- **New insights on causal inference in business** highlight why traditional causal methods need adaptation for practical decision-making.

---

## What Happened

OpenAI officially introduced **GPT-5.5**, a mid-generation upgrade that significantly boosts the Codex model’s performance on NVIDIA’s AI hardware stack. This means faster and more intelligent coding assistants and autonomous AI agents are becoming widely available.

Meanwhile, **DeepSeek-V4**, developed by Hugging Face, demonstrated an unprecedented million-token context window for language agents. This breakthrough allows handling entire books, multi-document sets, or long conversations in one go without losing context.

In the realm of applied data science, new discussions around **causal inference in business** emphasize the gap between academic causal methods and messy real-world business needs. The article stresses adapting causal thinking to business settings where experiments and assumptions are constrained.

---

## Why It Matters

- **GPT-5.5 and NVIDIA’s infrastructure** make high-performance AI tools more accessible and deployed at scale. For practitioners, it means better AI coding assistants, smarter automation, and more powerful agent-based workflows are now in reach.

- **Extended context windows** like DeepSeek-V4’s million-token model push the boundary for AI applications in documentation, legal analysis, research, and any domain requiring long-form understanding. This changes how we think about document summarization or multi-turn dialogue systems.

- **Bridging causal inference theory and practice** is critical for AI-driven decision-making tools. Business users often cannot run randomized trials or rely on ideal data. Methods that account for business constraints can improve AI impact and trustworthiness.
