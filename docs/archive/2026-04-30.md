## What Happened

### AI evals become compute bottleneck  
As AI models grow, evaluating their performance—running benchmarks, safety tests, and robustness checks—is consuming massive compute resources. Hugging Face’s recent blog explains that this evaluation overhead can exceed training costs, especially at scale. This shift is forcing teams to rethink workflow designs, optimize evaluation strategies, and invest in more cost-effective infrastructure.

### NVIDIA's Nemotron 3 Nano Omni model launch  
NVIDIA introduced Nemotron 3 Nano Omni, a unified large multimodal model that can handle vision, audio, and language inputs simultaneously. It’s designed for AI agents that operate more efficiently — promising up to a 9x speed and resource improvement. This innovation reflects the trend toward more capable, smaller, and more energy-efficient AI systems ready for real-world deployment.

### YAML files enable analyst-driven pipelines  
A practical approach described by Towards Data Science lets analysts build ETL and data pipelines using just 4 structured YAML files, eliminating the need for PySpark coding or heavy engineering involvement. This lowers the barrier for domain experts to manage pipelines directly, improving agility and collaboration between analysts and engineers.

---

## Why It Matters

- **Scaling AI models isn’t just about training — evaluation costs matter.** As evaluation takes a larger share of compute budgets, teams must innovate to manage costs, or risk bottlenecks that slow development cycles.
- **Multimodal models are the future of AI agents.** NVIDIA’s new model shows that combining multiple data types in one efficient architecture can unlock new applications and make AI agents more capable and cost-effective.
- **Empowering analysts reduces friction and cycle times.** Using declarative YAML files for pipelines democratizes data workflows, enabling faster iterations and reducing reliance on specialized engineering resources.

Together, these trends highlight a maturing AI ecosystem where efficiency, collaboration, and multimodal intelligence take center stage.

## Try This Today

1. **Review your AI evaluation workflows:**  
   Analyze your project’s eval budget. Are you running redundant or excessive benchmarks? Consider sampling strategies, caching reusable eval outputs, or batch processing to reduce compute.

2. **Explore YAML-driven pipelines:**  
   Try implementing a simple data transformation workflow using declarative YAML files instead of code. Frameworks like Dagster, Airflow (with YAML configs), or custom scripts can help you test this approach.

3. **Experiment with multimodal AI demos:**  
   Check out NVIDIA’s Nemotron 3 Nano Omni demos (if available) or similar multimodal playgrounds to see how vision, audio, and text data can be processed jointly. This can inspire ideas for new learning activities or projects.

## Teaching Angle

- **Focus on end-to-end AI lifecycle:** Use the evaluation compute bottleneck as a case study to teach not just model training but the equally critical step of model evaluation and deployment costs — giving students a more holistic picture of AI development.

- **Hands-on YAML pipelines:** Design a lab where students build simple data pipelines using YAML configurations. Highlight how declarative methods enable non-coders to contribute, reinforcing themes of collaboration between data engineers and analysts.

- **Multimodal AI integration:** Present NVIDIA’s Nemotron model to demonstrate how combining inputs from various modalities expands what AI can do. Encourage students to brainstorm real-world scenarios benefiting from multimodal inputs, such as robotics or immersive education.
