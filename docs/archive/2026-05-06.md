# Today in One Minute

- **NVIDIA and ServiceNow teamed up** to create autonomous AI agents designed to streamline enterprise workflows and reduce manual tasks.  
- **New modeling techniques** in Discrete Time-To-Event help predict *when* something will happen, not just *if*, improving event forecasting.  
- **OpenAI released GPT-5.5 Instant**, boasting smarter, clearer, and more personalized interactions with customizable system cards.

---

# What Happened

**NVIDIA and ServiceNow unveil AI agents for enterprises**  
NVIDIA announced a partnership with ServiceNow to develop autonomous AI agents that automate complex workflows in businesses. These agents combine NVIDIA’s AI computing power with ServiceNow’s workflow automation platform, enabling companies to reduce operational overhead and speed up decision-making.

**Discrete Time-To-Event Modeling explained**  
A new article breaks down discrete time-to-event (DTTE) modeling, a statistical approach that predicts *when* events occur over time, using machine learning to handle censored data and irregularly spaced events. This approach is particularly useful for predicting equipment failures, customer churn timing, or time-to-disease onset.

**OpenAI’s GPT-5.5 Instant launch**  
OpenAI rolled out GPT-5.5 Instant, an update featuring faster responses, enhanced clarity, and more personalized prompts via “system cards.” This lets users or developers tailor the AI’s tone, style, and task focus on the fly, improving real-world usability.

---

# Why It Matters

- **Enterprise AI agents** are evolving beyond chatbots to full workflow partners, boosting productivity by automating routine decisions and complex task coordination. This lowers the barrier for companies to adopt AI-driven process automation.
  
- **Time-to-event forecasting** moves AI from binary predictions (“Will a customer churn?”) to actionable insights about *when* such events might happen — key for proactive interventions in business and healthcare.
  
- **Customizable language models like GPT-5.5 Instant** show a shift toward more interactive, adaptable AI tools that can align better with user intent, enhancing both developer control and end-user satisfaction.
