## Today in One Minute

- **NVIDIA debuts Nemotron 3 Nano Omni**, a new multimodal AI model unifying vision, audio, and language for highly efficient AI agents—up to 9x faster.  
- **OpenAI expands availability** on AWS with Codex and managed agents, now FedRAMP Moderate certified, making secure deployment easier for enterprises and government.  
- **Chaos engineering enters AI production** as a key practice to increase reliability and robustness of complex AI systems under real-world stress.  

---

## What Happened

NVIDIA announced *Nemotron 3 Nano Omni*, a next-gen multimodal AI model designed to efficiently process and integrate vision, audio, and language inputs within a single agent architecture. This puts the spotlight on truly unified AI systems that can handle rich, diverse data streams in long contexts and at lower computational cost.

Meanwhile, OpenAI’s services, including Codex and managed AI agents, launched on AWS Marketplace with FedRAMP Moderate certification, paving the way for more compliant and scalable AI deployments in regulated environments such as government agencies and enterprises with strict security requirements.

On the operational side, the emerging discipline of **AI chaos engineering**—inspired by practices in cloud infrastructure reliability—is gaining attention. This involves intentionally introducing failures and perturbations in AI pipelines to identify weaknesses and improve fault tolerance before issues impact users.

---

## Why It Matters

- **Multimodal AI is the future**: Real-world AI applications rarely rely on just text or images alone. Nemotron 3 Nano Omni’s ability to process and jointly understand vision, sound, and language greatly enhances AI’s practical utility across industries, such as robotics, smart assistants, and content analysis.
  
- **Security and compliance unlock adoption**: OpenAI’s FedRAMP certification and AWS integration lower the barriers for regulated sectors to adopt advanced AI tools confidently, enabling productivity gains while meeting governance demands.

- **Reliability in AI systems is critical**: As AI moves from research labs to mission-critical production environments, robustness is key. Chaos engineering offers a systematic way to stress-test AI deployments, revealing hidden bugs or fragile components before failures happen, thereby improving user trust and system resilience.

## Try This Today

- **Experiment with multimodal AI apps**: Try building or exploring apps using NVIDIA’s Nemotron model (available via Hugging Face) to see how vision, audio, and language can be combined to solve complex tasks like multi-source document understanding or video analysis.

- **Deploy a secure OpenAI model on AWS**: If you have an AWS account, check out OpenAI’s Codex or managed agent offerings on the AWS Marketplace. Explore setting up a simple private AI assistant with compliance-ready infrastructure.

- **Introduce chaos engineering in your AI system**: Start small by simulating failures in data input streams or AI API latencies. See how your system responds. Document what breaks and think through mitigation strategies.

---

## Teaching Angle

- **Explain Multimodality with Examples**: Use NVIDIA Nemotron as a case study to demonstrate how AI can integrate multiple input types simultaneously for richer understanding. Practical exercises could involve designing simple apps that combine text, images, and audio.

- **Security Compliance in AI Deployment**: Discuss the importance of certifications like FedRAMP for deploying AI in sensitive environments. Have students map compliance requirements to design decisions in AI systems.

- **Chaos Engineering Basics for AI**: Introduce chaos engineering concepts with parallels to traditional software reliability. Run classroom “failure drills” on AI workflows to illustrate discovering and fixing hidden failure modes.
