## Today in One Minute

- **NVIDIA emphasizes a hybrid future**: AI innovation will blend open source and proprietary tech to accelerate capabilities and adoption.  
- **Human-in-the-loop workflows are evolving**: Practical, agentic systems that integrate human judgment with AI action are reshaping operational AI.  
- **New tools for developers**: LangChain advances with “Skills” in LangSmith Fleet and Moda’s production-grade AI design agents with Deep Agents, improving AI app building.

---

## What Happened

### 1. The Future of AI: Open Meets Proprietary  
NVIDIA’s blog outlines how the AI ecosystem will rely on both open and proprietary elements, balancing innovation speed with control and safety. They also announced donating a dynamic GPU resource allocation driver to the Kubernetes community, pushing AI infrastructure forward.

### 2. Practical Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Agentic Workflows  
An article from *Towards Data Science* explores building HITL workflows where humans guide AI agents that autonomously perform tasks but flag uncertain points for review, boosting efficiency and trustworthiness.

### 3. LangChain’s New “Skills” in LangSmith Fleet  
LangChain introduced “Skills”—modular, reusable AI capabilities that can be combined and managed at scale within LangSmith Fleet, making it easier to assemble complex agent behaviors without heavy custom engineering.

### 4. Moda’s Deep Agents for Production-Grade AI Design  
Moda demonstrated how to build reliable, scalable AI design agents by leveraging LangChain’s Deep Agents, creating more robust AI workflows for design automation.

---

## Why It Matters

- **Balancing openness and control** addresses real-world needs: While open source fosters innovation and collaboration, proprietary elements help secure IP and ensure quality, critical in enterprise AI.  
- **Human-in-the-loop workflows** tackle one of AI’s biggest pain points: scaling AI while maintaining human oversight protects against errors, bias, and unpredictable outputs.  
- **Modularity in AI development** (via Skills and Deep Agents) lowers barriers for practitioners by allowing reuse and faster iteration—key for rapid prototyping and deployment.  
- **Infrastructure improvements** (GPU drivers for Kubernetes) enable more efficient resource management, reducing costs and environmental impact of AI workloads.

## Try This Today

1. **Explore LangChain Skills**:

2. **Implement a Basic HITL Workflow**:

   Use Hugging Face’s guide to build or fine-tune embedding models for your domain in under a day. See how specialized embeddings improve retrieval or classification tasks compared to out-of-the-box models.

## Teaching Angle

- **Discuss the synergy between open and proprietary AI**: How does this hybrid model impact innovation, ethics, and enterprise adoption? Encourage debate about tradeoffs.  
- **Showcase Human-in-the-Loop best practices**: Use case studies or role-play scenarios where AI alone fails but HITL saves the day. Emphasize roles of human judgment and AI autonomy.  
- **Hands-on with LangChain Skills**: Build a mini-project in class where students create and combine reusable Skills, illustrating modular AI design principles.  
- **Highlight infrastructure’s invisible role**: Present how backend improvements like dynamic GPU allocation make AI scalable and sustainable, often overlooked but essential.
