# Today in One Minute

- New techniques in **causal inference** are improving how we measure real-world impacts, like how London tube strikes affect cycling habits.  
- OpenAI launched **workspace agents** in ChatGPT, enabling better multitasking and workflow automation for professionals, including clinicians.  
- NVIDIA and partners showcase **AI’s role in sustainability**, from rainforest conservation to recycling, highlighting AI’s growing environmental impact.

---

# What Happened

**Causal Inference Advances**  
Two articles from Towards Data Science explore how causal inference methods like propensity score matching are pushing beyond correlation to measure true impact in social and business settings. For example, estimating how tube strikes affect cycling in London by isolating cause-effect, not just correlation.

**AI-Enhanced Workflows**  
OpenAI introduced workspace agents in ChatGPT, allowing users to create specialised assistants that can access specific data, execute tasks repeatedly, and integrate smoothly into professional workflows. This includes improvements tailored to clinicians, helping them get more accurate, context-aware responses.

**AI for Sustainability and Industry**  
NVIDIA’s blog highlights AI projects protecting the environment—monitoring rainforests, optimizing recycling processes, and energy management. Additionally, NVIDIA and Google Cloud collaborate on agentic AI for smart factories, driving efficiency in production with physical AI agents.

---

# Why It Matters

- **Causal Inference in Practice**  
Understanding not just what is correlated but what causes change is critical for data-driven decision making. For example, city planners or product managers can allocate resources more effectively when they know the actual impact of events or interventions.

- **Automating and Scaling Productivity**  
Workspace agents turn ad hoc prompt experiments into repeatable, automated AI tasks. This is a step toward AI as an embedded co-worker rather than just a one-off tool, especially meaningful in complex fields like healthcare where precision matters.

- **AI’s Role Beyond Software**  
From sustainability to manufacturing, AI is increasingly embedding itself into physical systems. This expansion requires practitioners to design models and pipelines that consider real-world constraints and ethical implications alongside traditional software challenges.

# Try This Today

- **Experiment with Propensity Score Matching**  
Try implementing a propensity score matching technique on your existing dataset to identify true causal effects. For example, analyze A/B testing logs or user behavior changes around a product update.

- **Create a Workspace Agent**  
If you have access to ChatGPT’s workspace agents, build a simple assistant for a recurring task—such as summarizing daily logs, fetching specific data, or checking project status reports. Test how this automation can fit into your workflow.

- **Explore AI for Sustainability**  
Pick a small environmental dataset—energy usage, recycling data, or local pollution levels—and apply a simple AI model to uncover patterns or predict outcomes. This exercise connects AI skills with urgent global challenges.

---

# Teaching Angle

- **Distinguishing Correlation and Causation**  
Use examples from the tube strike and cycling usage studies to help students grasp why correlation does not equal causation. Practical exercises involving propensity score matching bring this concept to life.

- **Building Repeatable AI Workflows**  
Show how ad hoc prompting can develop into formal workflows as seen with Claude Code Skills and ChatGPT workspace agents. Assign a project where students automate a simple but repetitive data or text processing task using AI.

- **AI in the Physical World**  
Discuss the implications of AI operating in environments like factories or ecological systems. Encourage critical thought about safety, ethics, and the challenges of integrating AI with physical inputs and controls.
